Spatio-Temporal Distribution of Burned Areas

Comparative Analysis of VIIRS and MODIS MCD64 Data for Australia

Politecnico di Milano ¬∑ CNR-IREA ¬∑ Geoinformatics Engineering

Rishikesh Miriyala ¬∑ Sriram Krishnasamy Seenivasan ¬∑ Ghulam Abbas Zafari

Abstract

This study compares burned area estimates from global datasets (FireCCI54 and MCD64) using Google Earth Engine. Multi-year analysis reveals spatial and temporal patterns, evaluates the influence of climate and land use, and integrates various environmental indicators to enhance fire risk assessment and dataset reliability.

This report presents an in-depth analysis of the comparison between spatio-temporal burned area distributions derived from global burned area products: VIIRS and MODIS MCD64, with a focus on Australia.

Project Overview

Our project aims to compare multi-annual burned area estimates obtained from VIIRS and MODIS MCD64, two major satellite-based burned area datasets. The objective is to analyze the spatio-temporal distribution of burned areas and assess the impact of climatic zones, land cover types, and other factors influencing fire occurrence.

Methodology

We implemented temporal and spatial aggregation methods using Google Earth Engine (GEE) and Python, analyzing data from 2019 to 2023. The following steps were followed:

  • Data Collection: Access multi-annual global datasets via GEE and filter/clip datasets to the region of interest (ROI): Australia.
  • Spatial Aggregation: Aggregate data by country, land cover type, biome, and climatic zone.
  • Temporal Aggregation: Create time series of NDVI patterns to monitor vegetation recovery post-fire. Assess burned area trends over the period 2019‚Äì2023.
  • Analysis: Perform statistical analysis and visualize results using Python.

Key Findings

  • Wildfire Trends: A noticeable increase in burned areas during certain seasons, particularly in Australia's temperate and tropical biomes.
  • Comparison of Products: Differences observed in burned area estimates between FireCCI51 and MODIS MCD64, with FireCCI51 showing slightly higher estimates.
  • Climatic Impact: Fires in arid regions showed distinct patterns compared to temperate zones, with higher seasonality and more pronounced peak months.
