Welcome to the Geo-Informatic Project

Bringing Geospatial Data to Life with Interactive Mapping

Introduction

The Spatio-Temporal distribution of burned areas (BA) is critical for understanding fire dynamics and their ecological, climatic, and socio-economic impacts. This study presents a comprehensive comparison of burned area estimates derived from major global datasets including FireCCI54 and MCD64. Using multi-annual datasets and the Google Earth Engine (GEE) platform, we analyze the spatial and temporal patterns of burned areas at global and regional scales.

Custom-developed algorithms implemented in GEE facilitate the comparison of products, ensuring consistent and accurate assessments. The findings provide insights into the variability and reliability of BA datasets, highlighting implications for their use in fire management. This research contributes to advancing methodologies for inter-product comparison and improving the robustness of global burned area monitoring systems.

Keywords

Burned Area (BA), Spatio-Temporal Analysis, Global BA Products, FireCCI54, MCD64, Multi-Annual Analysis, Google Earth Engine (GEE)
